# SleepySheep ASMR Redesign Plan

Date: 2026-07-16

Status: planning only; no implementation changes made.

## Objective

Redesign the ASMR catalog so it feels like a set of immersive, sleep-oriented ambience experiences rather than a collection of generic sound-type labels. The new catalog should preserve the Dreamy worldbuilding while making each preset more distinct, more comforting, and more aligned with the actual generated audio.

## Design direction

### Core identity

SleepySheep ASMR should feel like:
- gentle nighttime ambience
- cozy bedtime spaces
- soft emotional comfort
- dreamy, low-stimulation relaxation

The catalog should not feel like a technical sound library. It should feel like a small world of peaceful scenes.

## Proposed catalog structure

### 1. SleepySheep Signature

These are the flagship scenes and should anchor the catalog.

1. Dreamy Meadow
   - quiet meadow at night
   - distant sheep ambience
   - gentle air and soft natural hush
   - purpose: calm, spacious, and emotionally safe

2. Moonlight Ranch
   - peaceful ranch ambience
   - soft breeze and distant nighttime textures
   - subtle rural comfort
   - purpose: warm and familiar, but still quiet

3. Cozy Hearth
   - fireplace warmth
   - indoor nighttime comfort
   - gentle crackle and soft room tone
   - purpose: strong emotional coziness

4. Dreamlike Atmosphere
   - dreamy ambient texture
   - low, floating, sleep-inducing soundscape
   - minimal and immersive
   - purpose: direct sleep support and emotional softness

### 2. Cozy Spaces

These scenes add domestic comfort and gentle narrative depth.

5. Tea House
   - quiet indoor retreat
   - soft rain or room tone
   - warm, intimate, calm environment
   - purpose: a refined cozy space rather than a generic rain preset

6. Library Evening
   - soft page-turn and room hush
   - calm, reflective, late-night energy
   - purpose: peaceful and thoughtful, not overly active

### 3. Nature and Nighttime Atmosphere

These are supporting scenes and should be fewer and more intentional.

7. Zen Garden
   - water, wind, and bells
   - serene and balanced
   - purpose: brief meditation-like calm

8. Forest Camp
   - quiet campfire ambience with forest air
   - gentle night texture
   - purpose: immersive and earthy without being loud

## Catalog reduction strategy

### Consolidate duplicates

The redesign should merge entries that are effectively the same experience:

- Rain + Rainy Night -> Tea House or a more specific rainy indoor scene
- Ocean + Ocean Night -> Dreamy Meadow or a coastal variation if desired later
- River + Water Drops -> Zen Garden
- Fire + Cabin + Cozy preset + Cottage preset -> Cozy Hearth or Forest Camp
- Brown noise + Deep Sleep -> Dreamlike Atmosphere
- Zen Garden + Zen preset -> Zen Garden
- Traditional instrument preset -> Tea House or Zen Garden, depending on tone

### Remove or demote generic variants

The following should be removed from the primary catalog or reserved for a later bonus section:
- generic white noise
- generic brown noise
- pure pulse/tingle textures
- overly technical or sound-effect-like entries

These do not support the SleepySheep scene-first identity as strongly as atmosphere-based presets.

## Naming and tone guidance

The catalog should use names that feel:
- warm
- soft
- story-like
- bedtime-oriented
- emotionally comforting

Preferred tone examples:
- Dreamy Meadow
- Moonlight Ranch
- Cozy Hearth
- Dreamlike Atmosphere
- Tea House
- Library Evening
- Zen Garden
- Forest Camp

This is stronger than naming entries after isolated sound types such as Rain, Stream, or Water Drops.

## Recommended catalog size

The main catalog should be kept compact, ideally around 8 entries.

That size is enough to feel varied while still staying coherent and memorable.

## Recommended implementation order

1. Replace the current generic entries with the 8 scene-based presets above.
2. Merge overlapping sound generators into the new scene definitions.
3. Keep descriptions tightly aligned with the actual audible experience.
4. Preserve the existing SleepySheep visual and emotional tone in the card labels and descriptions.

## Expected outcome

After the redesign:
- the catalog will feel more cohesive
- each entry will feel more distinctive
- the experience will better support sleep and relaxation
- the SleepySheep identity will become clearer and more memorable
